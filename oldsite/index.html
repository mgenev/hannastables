Welcome to Hanna Stables and Nabitunich (The Stone Cottage)

Glad you could drop in and explore the many activities we have to offer! Hanna Stables is located between two prestigious towns of Western Belize. We are nestled within 400 acres of lush green spread of rolling farm land, garden of orchids, roses and fruit trees. We offer a unique experience; surrounded by forested areas, open fields, Mayan temples and long bridle paths. We are able to customize our rides to suit all our guests' needs.
Hanna's Stables, located at mile 70 on the Western Highway caters for all riding levels from the inexperienced rider; who just wants to enjoy a leisurely ride, to the experienced more adventurous riders. Our rides allow us to encounter wonderful birds, rodents, lizards and small mammals. A day at the farm can be an unforgettable adventure with untouched rainforest surrounds the farm, beautiful free flowing river for tubing, canoeing or just hike and bike.


Nabitunich
Welcome to Nabitunich, "The Little Stone Cottage" in Maya, a world nestled at the foot of the great Mayan temple of XUNANTUNICH, which means 'The Lady of Stone'. The Stone Cottage is located on the Western Highway, 70 miles from Belize City and 3 miles from the Guatemalan border. The cottages are a ten minute walk from the entrance. Each cottage has two bedrooms, which can accommodate two or three people.

Endurance Horse Racing
Hanna's Stables are the founding pioneers in endurance and competitive trail riding within Belize. In the competitive trail riding sport; riders cover a marked trail for a specified distance. The distance may vary from 10 to 30 plus miles per a single event. The goal of the competition is to exhibit partner-ship between rider and the horse. While in the endurance riding many factors are taken into consideration other than speed. The horse is normally evaluated on pacing, rider's behavior and "Pulse and Respiration" (the recovery ability and conditioning of the horse). VIEW VEDIO CLIPS BELOW
Archeological View of Nabitunich
Nabitunich is located in a place that people have called home for thousands of years. The region's early inhabitant's hunted wild game, caught fish in the river, and harvested wild fruits, nuts, and tubers beginning by at least 12,000 years ago. By 1000 BC, people in the valley were growing crops like corn and beans in living a settled lifestyle in small villages at Xunantunich, Cahal Pech, and other sites.
