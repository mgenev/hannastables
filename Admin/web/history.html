History of Nabitunich

NABITUNICH, which means ‘Little Stone House”, is situated about a mile from the ancient Mayan Temple of Xunantunich, which means Maiden of Stone.

The first cottage at Nabitunich was built of stone quarried at San Lorenzo farm and thatch made with local palm leaf.

The original cottage was built in early 1985 and was quickly followed by several more cottages and a dining room. These were used exclusively by doctors and nurses who came to work at the Good Shepherd Clinic in San Jose Succotz.

Lying almost at the center of the 400 acre farm of San Lorenzo, which has been in the Juan family for four generations, the location offers a wide variety of outdoor pursuits to visitors and family alike. Birding, botany, swimming on boating on the lovely Mopan River which meanders through the farm or discovering the multitude of historical mounds, evidence of Mayan civilizations are but a few of the interesting ways to spend hours or even weeks.

Eventually, it was decided to open the cottages to the general public. It was therefore on 23rd December 1987, that our very first non medical guests arrived: Professors and students from the Milwaukee Zoo and Botanical gardens.

As it was also our daughters 15th birthday, the guests all joined in the celebration and danced to the music of the local marimba.

As guests began returning each year, many became interested in seeing more of the area on horseback. So riding became popular and guests began to enjoy even greater exposure to a different way of life.

As Nabitunich approaches its 26th year, we look back on many happy times, we remember honeymoon couples, weddings, professional groups, students and people from all walks of life; making lifetime friendships with man.

We look forward to a future, ever changing but ever constant in the things that matter most.

We look forward to your visit. Come and experience Belize’s original Home Away from Home.
